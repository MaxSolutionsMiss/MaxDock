MaxDock v64-DB43 — Operational Console Density

Purpose
- Move MaxDock from a decorative landing-page treatment toward a calm, compact shipping-and-receiving console.
- Reduce toolbar and table height while preserving readable controls and mobile touch targets.
- Make frequent navigation visible on desktop and keep role filtering intact.

Dashboard and Operations Queue
- Schedule Date, Appointment Status, and Date Range use inline label-to-field pairs on desktop.
- Operating Date and Queue View use the same inline pattern in Operations Queue.
- Schedule, Display, and Documents group captions are removed from the operational toolbars.
- Display settings, Print, Export CSV, full-screen, date shortcuts, and Refresh share one compact toolbar row where the viewport allows.
- Tablet and mobile layouts wrap cleanly and retain larger touch targets.

Operational Design System
- Page background effects are replaced by a quiet solid workspace background.
- Panels, KPI cards, queue cards, and configuration surfaces use flat white surfaces, one-pixel borders, and consistent 8–10 pixel radii.
- Decorative gradients and glow are removed from routine controls and schedule states.
- Navy, blue, teal, and functional status colors remain the identity and operating signals.
- Menus, login, and the primary Place Appointment action retain restrained elevation where it communicates layering or priority.

Tables
- Floating card rows are replaced with a single bordered table surface.
- Row gaps, per-row corner radii, and decorative elevation are removed.
- Headers, cells, dividers, zebra striping, and hover feedback are tightened for faster scanning.
- General table minimum width is reduced while the nine-column Dashboard and User Management tables retain appropriate operational minimums.
- Status tags use compact flat shapes.

Navigation and Workspaces
- Authenticated desktop pages gain persistent role-aware navigation for Appointments, Queue, Dashboard, Reports, Settings, User Management, and Data Integration.
- Existing permission code remains the source of truth; links hidden by role are also hidden in persistent navigation.
- The gear menu remains available for narrow screens, secondary destinations, account controls, and complete navigation.
- Settings, Data Integration, Reports, and My Appointments use the left-navigation/right-workspace pattern where a real view choice exists.
- Sidebar headings use a distinct shaded header band so they are visually separate from selectable menu items.

Customer Entry and Login
- Place Appointment returns to a visually prominent navy-blue-teal action and is approximately 25 percent larger than DB42.
- The customer entry panel remains simpler than the earlier glass-and-glow treatment.
- Login and password setup use a compact MaxDock product wordmark with “Max Solutions Packaging Inc.” as secondary company attribution.

Architecture Reconciliation
- DB33 through DB36 were not dropped in DB42. They are dynamically loaded by maxdock-config.js together with the later refinement layers.
- DB43 preserves that layered no-build architecture and loads after DB42.
- Repository memory now records DB42 as production and DB43 as the active reviewed release.

Database and Edge Functions
- No Supabase migration is required.
- No Edge Function deployment is required.
- Scheduling, appointment, role, RLS, live-refresh, capacity, MIS, and customer-confidentiality behavior is unchanged.

Release Delivery
- Publish matching root and db04 copies.
- Browser assets use the unique v64-db43 cache marker.
- The menu displays “DB43 · operational console density active”.
